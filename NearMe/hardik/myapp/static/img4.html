Tenkasi

Kasi Viswanathar Temple is one of the most famous temples in Tenkasi. The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva, who is worshipped here as Kasi Viswanathar. It was built by King Parakrama Pandyan during the 15th century and is known for its beautiful Dravidian-style architecture. The tall Rajagopuram with detailed carvings is a major attraction for visitors and devotees. The name “Tenkasi” means “Southern Kasi,” as the temple is considered similar to the famous Kasi temple in Varanasi. Many pilgrims visit the temple to seek blessings for peace, health, and prosperity. Festivals like Maha Shivaratri are celebrated grandly here, attracting thousands of devotees every year. The temple is also located near popular tourist places such as Courtallam Falls, making it an important spiritual and tourist destination.
